Gap 1 of 3 — The Root Cause

Relevance & Frequency

Roku has 100M households of first-party data — but viewers still see the same ad 4× an hour across apps with no OS-level cap in place.

The Core Problem

Frequency capping on Roku operates at the publisher level — not the OS level. A viewer watching Tubi, The Roku Channel, and Peacock in one evening can see the same Progressive ad 9 times — once per break per app — with no cross-platform cap in place.

Roku's own 2026 predictions acknowledge this: viewers will "revolt with their remotes" against egregious ad loads. Yet Roku's first-party data advantage — 145B streaming hours of viewing habits — is barely surfacing in the relevance of ads those viewers actually see.

This is the root cause gap. Fixing frequency and relevance at the OS layer unlocks everything downstream.

Seeing the same insurance commercial four times in one hour makes a viewer feel like the channel is broken — or deliberately disrespectful of their time.

Opportunity 1

Intelligent Ad Frequency at the OS Layer

The Big Idea
Roku uses its unique OS-level position to enforce cross-app frequency caps and serve relevance signals that no individual publisher can replicate.
Turning Roku's data moat into a viewer experience moat — and a competitive advantage no Amazon or Samsung can easily replicate without OS-level control.
🛡️Cross-App Frequency Cap
OS-level intelligence caps ad repetition across all Roku apps. Viewer sees a given creative max 2× per session regardless of which app served it. No competitor without OS-level control can match this.
🎯Relevance Score Engine
Surface Roku's first-party data (viewing history, genre affinity, time-of-day behavior) to advertisers in Ads Manager as a targeting signal — making campaigns smarter without requiring additional creative production.
🔄Creative Diversity Enforcement
Require minimum creative rotation thresholds for campaigns above a set frequency — incentivizing advertisers to build 2–3 variants and automatically reducing viewer fatigue at scale.
⚠ Tradeoff to Navigate
Publisher contracts may guarantee minimum ad fill rates — OS-level capping could force Roku to absorb unsold inventory or renegotiate deals with FAST partners like Tubi and Peacock. Resolution: tiered rollout starting with The Roku Channel (owned-and-operated), then expand to third-party publishers with revised fill guarantees backed by viewer retention data.
How I'd Validate This
→A/B test OS-level frequency cap (3×/session) vs. status quo on 500K households — measure session length, ad completion rate, and 30-day platform retention
→Hypothesis: cap reduces irritation without measurably reducing advertiser reach at the 3× threshold — what does the data say?
→Proxy metric: does a 10% improvement in ad experience NPS correlate with measurable lift in publisher retention and household ARPU?

Gap 2 of 3 — The Missed Opportunity

Interactive Format Access

Shoppable ads drive 47% higher purchase intent — but 90% of Ads Manager advertisers can't use them due to the creative production barrier.

The Core Problem

Roku's Action Ads and shoppable formats are proven — 118× more likely remote engagement than QR, 47% higher purchase intent, 45% of purchasers new-to-brand.

But they require finished video creative and production sophistication that small and mid-market advertisers on Roku Ads Manager simply don't have. The result: Roku's most powerful ad format is effectively reserved for enterprise brands.

Now Samsung + Amazon are launching equivalent shoppable formats in July 2026. The window to lock in mid-market advertisers before competitors do is closing fast.

It wasn't so long ago that people said no one would shop on the internet. Then no one would shop from their phones. In shoppable TV, Roku would like a word.

Opportunity 2

Democratize Interactive Formats for the Mid-Market

The Big Idea
Embed AI-powered creative tools directly into Roku Ads Manager — removing the production bottleneck that prevents mid-market advertisers from accessing Action Ads.
Before Samsung + Amazon's July 2026 shoppable launch, lock in the mid-market advertiser base with interactive formats they can activate in minutes, not weeks.
🤖AI Creative Studio
Embed GenAI video creation into Ads Manager — advertisers input their URL or brand brief, AI generates a CTV-ready 15 or 30-sec spot with interactive overlay. No production team needed.
🧩Interactive Overlay Templates
Pre-built Action Ad templates (OK to Shop, Send to Phone, QR) that any advertiser can attach to existing video creative with one click. Interactive without rebuilding from scratch.
🤝Creator × Brand Studio
Co-branded native ad units pairing Roku creator content with brand messaging in one interactive format. Lower cost, higher authenticity, new inventory type for the platform.

Gap 3 of 3 — The Broken Feedback Loop

Attribution & Real-Time Measurement

Advertisers from search and social expect instant ROAS signals. Roku's measurement lag risks budget flight to Amazon DSP.

36%
of CTV budget increases redirected from social — expect real-time loops
3×

18%

The Core Problem

36% of new CTV advertisers are redirecting budgets from social; 32% from search. These buyers are used to Meta and Google's near-real-time optimization — adjust creative, see results in 24 hours, reallocate budget.

Roku's measurement — improving through iSpot and INCRMNTAL — still requires longer optimization windows. Cross-device attribution is murky. ROAS signals are delayed.

For mid-market advertisers who can't afford to wait weeks to know if a campaign worked, this friction is a reason not to scale — or a reason to choose Amazon DSP's authenticated graph instead.

Measuring the true impact of CTV has historically been tough. Roku's Ads API lets measurement partners sync delivery data in real time and power advanced measurement.

Opportunity 3

Close the Measurement Gap for the Mid-Market

The Big Idea
Build a real-time performance dashboard inside Roku Ads Manager — giving mid-market advertisers the ROAS confidence they had on Meta and Google.
Surfacing campaign results within 24 hours so advertisers can optimize and scale, rather than wait and stall — or worse, move budget to Amazon DSP.
📊Real-Time ROAS Dashboard
Surface reach, frequency, completion rate, attributed site visits, and estimated ROAS within 24 hours in Ads Manager. Built on the Roku Ads API launched November 2025 — infrastructure already exists.
🔬Self-Serve Incrementality Reports
Expose INCRMNTAL partnership data to Ads Manager users as an automated post-campaign report. First platform to give SMB advertisers TV incrementality without a managed service contract.
🔗Cross-Device Attribution Link
Use Roku's authenticated household graph to connect TV ad exposure to downstream mobile and web actions — a single attribution line across screens without third-party cookies.
Estimated Opportunity
$15–30M
incremental annual revenue from improved advertiser retention
Conservative — retention data not publicly disclosed

Assumptions

1
Ads Manager advertiser base: 15,000+ active accounts (Roku targets 20,000+ new streaming advertisers in 2025)
2
Current 90-day retention estimated ~40–50% for 1-campaign advertisers (industry benchmark for self-serve CTV)
3
Target: lift 90-day retention by 10pp — supported by Meta's 2018 Ads Manager redesign achieving ~12pp lift via real-time reporting
4
Average second-campaign spend: $2,000–$5,000 for SMB advertisers (IAB 2025 + Roku $500 minimum)
Most sensitive variable: the undisclosed Ads Manager advertiser count. At 10K accounts + $2K avg, floor is $20M. At 20K accounts + $3K avg, approaches $60M.
⚠ Tradeoff to Navigate
CTV attribution models are inherently imperfect. Overstated ROAS estimates erode advertiser trust when results don't materialize. Mitigation: label all figures as modeled with confidence intervals; use incrementality reports as the higher-credibility causal signal; never present modeled ROAS as deterministic truth.
How I'd Validate This
→Beta real-time dashboard with 300 Ads Manager advertisers who ran 1–2 campaigns and didn't return — measure re-activation rate vs control group at 60 days
→Does next-day performance data increase avg campaign budget by ≥20% in 30 days following first campaign? That's the proxy for measurement confidence unlocking spend
→Compare advertiser 90-day retention (Roku Ads Manager vs Amazon DSP self-serve) before and after dashboard launch — is the gap closing?
